Dr. Dennis Schwarz

I am an experimental particle physicist working as a PostDoc at the HEPHY in Vienna. I am interested in foundamental questions of our universe and try to shine light on things we do not yet understand. As a member of the CMS Collaboration at the CERN LHC, I am working on measurements connected to the top quark, the heaviest know elementary particle.

CV

since 2022 Convener for the CMS ttX group
since 2021 PostDoc at HEPHY, Vienna
since 2017 Member of the CMS Collaboration
2017-2021 PhD at University of Hamburg

During my PhD at the University of Hamburg, my main project was a measurement of the jet mass in hadronic decays of boosted top quarks. In addition, I worked on the calibration of top tagging algorithms. During my PostDoc at the HEPHY in Vienna, I extended my portfolio to EFT interpretations with top quarks and pairs of electroweak bosons. I am also exploring novel techniques to measure the top quark mass using energy correlators.
