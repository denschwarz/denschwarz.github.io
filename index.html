Dr. Dennis Schwarz

0000-0002-3821-7331

I am an experimental particle physicist working as a PostDoc at DESY in Hamburg. I am interested in fundamental questions of our universe and try to shine light on phenomena we do not yet understand. As a member of the CMS Collaboration at the CERN LHC, I am working on measurements connected to the top quark, the heaviest know elementary particle.

CV

since 2025 Research Fellow at DESY, Hamburg
2022-2024 Convener for the CMS ttX group
2021-2025 PostDoc at HEPHY, Vienna
since 2017 Member of the CMS Collaboration
2017-2021 PhD, University of Hamburg
2015-2017 Master's Degree in Physics, University of Hamburg
2011-2015 Bachelor's Degree in Physics, University of Hamburg

During my PhD at the University of Hamburg, my main project was a measurement of the jet mass in hadronic decays of boosted top quarks. In addition, I worked on the calibration of top tagging algorithms. During my PostDoc at the HEPHY in Vienna, I extended my portfolio to EFT interpretations with top quarks and pairs of electroweak bosons. I am also exploring novel techniques to measure the top quark mass using energy correlators.

Outreach

As a scientist, funded by public resources, I consider outreach an essential part of my role. In the last years I have been organizing projects targeting pupils (e.g. CERN Masterclasses, Physik Projekt Tage), giving public lectures (Wiener Volkshochschule), or explaining my research in local bars and pubs (Pint of Science).
